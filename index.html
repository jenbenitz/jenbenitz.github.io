JENNIFER BENITZ

RECENT GRAD APPLIES FOR JOB

My name is Jennifer Benitz. I am a recent graduate of Pennsylvania State University currently based in the Washington D.C. area. The benefits I have experienced from effective reporting, together with the ability to thrive under pressure and a passion for writing, have inspired me to follow a career in journalism.

Journalism has progressed from a traditional print platform to a dynamic online presence, particularly with the influence of social media. In a similar fashion, I have updated my resume to show the progressive vision and creativity I can bring. Thank you for visiting my web site.

Aspiring journalist ready to write, edit and discover.

OBJECTIVE

My English degree has sharpened my analytical and writing skills, while previous experiences have given me a diligent work ethic, a keen eye for detail and the ability to adapt quickly in changing environments. I want to pursue a career of respectable and effective reporting, while continually refining my writing skills and expanding my knowledge base. I want to explore different platforms of journalism, including print and broadcast. I want to learn from experienced professionals in the field while challenging my journalistic abilities and communication skills.

EDUCATION

PENNSYLVANIA STATE UNIVERSITY

2008-2012

I earned my Bachelors Degree at Penn State University in May of 2012 with a Major in English and a Minor in Psychology.

As part of my English education, I learned a variety of writing and literature styles, including:

  • Editorial Process;
  • Technical writing;
  • Biographical writing;
  • Expository writing; and
  • Nonfiction writing.

As a student of the College of Liberal Arts, I had the freedom to shape my curriculum during my final two years, choosing to focus on writing and journalism.

I chose to study Psychology to understand a person’s thought process and how people interact with each other. This has been especially useful in my journalistic work when interviewing people and analyzing situations.

JOURNALISM

CONNECTION NEWSPAPER

2012

After searching for an internship in newspaper job section, I came across Connection Newspapers, one of the oldest newspapers in the country. I applied and got a position as an Intern.

I primarily reported for Great Falls and traveled as far south as Lorton, covering community events, residents and news. I managed their website, copy editing and acquired valuable knowledge of the production process.

COLLEGE OF AGRICULTURAL SCIENCES

2011-2012

I was recommended by the College of Liberal Arts to be Writer for Penn State’s College of Agricultural Sciences. My goal was to develop skills for both print and electronic journalism.

Upon completion of the internship, I had written more stories in a single semester than any other intern in the program’s ten year existence.

EDITORIAL

FOXDALE PROJECT

2011-2012

In my junior and senior year at Penn State, I served as Editor on the Foxdale Project. The purpose of this project was for students to write biographies on local residents who were 90 and older.

My role consisted of copy-editing, page layout design and writing. Over the course of two semesters, I edited over 50 biographies and prepared them for publication.

KALLIOPE MAGAZINE

2011-2012

As a member of the nonfiction reading staff for Kalliope, Penn State’s Literary Magazine, I gained valuable experience in the analysis and selection process of literary works. As a team member, I had direct contact with the editor and the total of 13 submissions were selected for publication for the nonfiction section of the magazine.

LEADERSHIP

IFC/PANHELLENIC DANCE MARATHON

2010-2012

As a volunteer for the largest student-run philanthropy in the world, Penn State’s IFC / Panhellenic Dance Marathon (THON), I served as a key member for the operations committee.

Over the course of my three-year involvement in THON, we raised over $28 million while providing emotional support for the children of Hershey Medical Center in Hershey, PA.

ADOPT-A-SCHOOL

2011-2012

During my three years with Adopt-A-School, I served as treasurer and Family Relations Chair, managing the club’s finances and communication between club members and our THON family.
